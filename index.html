Intro

Belgrade has always been a meeting point.
In terms of geography,Belgrade is the place
where is the Pannonian Plain meets the
mountainous Balkans and where the rivers
Sava and Danube meet,while culturally, the
east and the west have been intertwining
in Belgrade for centuries. Today, this is a
modern capital, which, although fast-paced,
proudly preserves its rich historical heritage.
Maybe this exactly is the reason why Belgrade is
open to visitors, and why the locals are
friendly and helpful.

Locals

If you ask anybody who's been to Belgrade what made
the strongest impression on them in the city, most
people will say that it's the city atmosphere, that is,
the people. It is thanks to them that Belgrade secured
its placeon the list of European tourist attractions.
People living in Belgrade are distinctive continental
Mediterraneans - open, talktative, and charming
outsiders.They make it impossible for you to get lost
in the city, while simple questions about transport
or a city desination may lead to going out for a drink
and a lifelong friendship.

Palaces

After achieving independance in 1878, culture and economy
began to flourish in Serbia, and during this period
until the Second World War, 4 palaces were built in Belgrade:
the Old Palace,New palace,Royal palace,and White
Palace.The Old and New Palace, grandiose buildings, combining elemets
of the clasical style, renaissance, and
baroque are located in the very center of the city,while the Royal and
White Palace are in Dedinje, within the wonderful
Royal Compound, which includes pergolas, park terraces, swimming
pools, pavilions, and a concert terrace.

Nightlife

Nightlife in the serbian capital has been the subject
of many international media for a long time, and the
city is often compared to Berlin due to its vibrant nightlife.
Belgrade can boast of its incredible big
and diverse nightlife scene.One of the advantages of
Belgrade nightlife is that it's centralized.Most places
are distributed among these three locations: Cetinjska
Street, Savamala, and floating clubs in Ušće,but
there are also those located in some other remoter
locations.In just a few hours, you can experience many
different settings, atmospheres, and music genres,
without much effort and without spending too much
money on taxi, and have a night to remember.

Gastronomy

A part of getting to know Belgrade and Serbian culture definetly
ivolves trying specialties of Serbian cuisine.Food preparation is a
special part of Serbian tradition and culture, and Serbian cuisine is
famous for diverse, rich and spicy food, which can be described as a
combination of Greek, Turkish, and Hungarian cuisine.There are
several specialties which you simply must try:

Čvarci(Pork cracklings)

It's hard to describe Čvarci and the method of preparation,
which is a long and a hard job, but once you have a taste of "tobacco" pork cracklings or regular cracklings, you
will realize that the recipe for this unique meat product
should indeed remain a secret, since the flavor is unique.
Čvarci are an indispensable appetizer in Serbia.

Wine

Serbia has an interesting wine scene and it keeps
getting better and more diverse with each year, so
make the best of your stay in Belgrade and try wines
made by local producers.Pay special attention to
winesfrom Grocka, a nearby place on the Danube,
where you can find wine grape varieties originating
from Belgrade wine region, encompassing the areas of
Avala and Kosmaj mountains, Lazarevac and Mladenovac.

Ćevapi(Meat fingers)

Ćevapi are made of ground meat, rolled into a
shape of tiny sausages and grilled.They are
traditionally eaten with onion and kaymak, and
are often served within flatbread.

Rakija

Rakija is an alchoholic beverage made of almost any
type of fruit, but the most popular are those made of
plum, quince, pear, apricot and wine.In Serbian culture,
rakija is a symbol of hospitality.A rakija bottle
is one of the most popular souvenirs of Serbia.
